See a lot more about every port

WhatPort now surfaces much more of what your Mac already knows about each USB-C port, adds a proper right-click menu, and fixes a couple of bugs.

## New

- **Display detail.** For a connected screen, see the link rate (HBR2 / HBR3), how many lanes it is using, whether it is native DisplayPort or tunnelled through Thunderbolt, how many displays are attached, and any hub or HDMI converter in the chain.
- **"Why doesn't my dock work?"** A new Connections section shows exactly what a port has set up (USB 2, USB 3, DisplayPort, and so on) and flags anything macOS has blocked, so you know to approve the accessory in Settings.
- **Names for your gear.** Connected Thunderbolt docks are named, and your charger is identified with its full power menu, for example "Supports up to 96 W (5 V, 9 V, 15 V, 20 V)".
- **Liquid detection.** If your Mac detects liquid in a port, WhatPort shows a clear warning and what to do about it.
- **Right-click menu and About.** Right-click the menu bar icon for Settings, WhatPort on GitHub, About, and Quit. Settings and Quit also work with the standard ⌘, and ⌘Q shortcuts, and there is a new About card.

## Fixes

- **No more duplicate icon.** Turning on "Launch at Login" no longer leaves you with two WhatPort icons in the menu bar.
- **Settings scrolls.** The settings panel no longer gets clipped, with the Back button pushed off-screen, when the Flight Recorder sections are present.

## Install

Download below, unzip, and drag WhatPort.app to Applications, or:

```
brew upgrade --cask whatport
```

The app is signed and notarised by Apple.
