Correctness fixes for port identity, power, and readability

This release is all fixes, no new features. Most of them came out of testing against real hardware.

## Fixes

- **Thunderbolt 5 ports now read as TB5.** They were being labelled TB4. The port's true capability now comes from the controller's Thunderbolt version, which is reported even when nothing is plugged in.
- **The menu bar popover is readable on dark backgrounds.** On macOS Tahoe the Liquid Glass material left the panel almost see-through over a dark desktop. It now has a solid backing in both light and dark mode. (Thanks to Chris Messina for the report.)
- **Power shows up on the right port.** A charger and its power details now attach to the port the charger is actually on. A USB-C cable with nothing on the other end no longer claims to be charging or shows "Battery Full".
- **Charger details on Macs without per-port metering.** Some Macs (such as the base M5) do not expose per-port power-out metering. The negotiated charger contract and live draw are now shown on those machines instead of a blank "unavailable".
- **USB devices map to the correct physical port.** The physical ports and any connected USB devices are now tied together by the controller's stable per-port identity, so a device on a higher-numbered port no longer goes missing.
- **USB 3 links blocked by macOS are shown as blocked.** A link that macOS has restricted (an unapproved accessory) used to look like a healthy idle port. It now says so, and points at where to approve it.

## Install

Download below, unzip, and drag WhatPort.app to Applications, or:

```
brew upgrade --cask whatport
```

The app is signed and notarised by Apple.
