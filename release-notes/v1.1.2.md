The Flight Recorder, and a more accurate free app

The headline is the Flight Recorder (Pro): always-on background recording of what your USB-C ports are doing, so you can catch the intermittent faults that only show up when nobody's watching. The free app also reads power more accurately and surfaces a few things sooner.

## New: the Flight Recorder (Pro)

- **Always-on history.** Records each port's power, protocol, and link state to a local database every few seconds, survives restarts, flat memory.
- **Survives an overnight kill.** With Launch at Login on, restarts itself within seconds if macOS stops it to free memory. A deliberate Quit still quits.
- **"While you were away."** Tells you what changed since you last looked, and flags any recording gap instead of letting it read as quiet.
- **Timeline, graphs, and per-event detail.** A selectable time range, an all-ports power graph, and the full captured state for any event.
- **Sessions.** Bookmark a stretch of recording, name it, and export it to JSON or CSV.
- **Port health.** Scored by how often faults recur, with a clear "not a hardware diagnosis" note. Acknowledge counters to reset the baseline.
- **Alerts.** Notify on overcurrent, link error, disconnect, or protocol downgrade.

## Improvements

- **More accurate power.** Sourced from the SMC, split into in and out.
- **Protocol and speed sooner.** Shown on connect, with USB/DisplayPort speed-change detection.

## Updating from an earlier version

WhatPort's internal identifier changed (the old one was a placeholder). macOS treats the update as a new app, so if you had Launch at Login switched on under an older version, you may find a leftover "WhatPort" entry in System Settings ▸ General ▸ Login Items. Remove it with the minus button. The new version manages its own.

## Install

Download below, unzip, and drag WhatPort.app to Applications, or:

```
brew upgrade --cask whatport
```

The app is signed and notarised by Apple.
